# Hero Emblem: Generation and Ingest Record

- **Tool:** Google Gemini (image generation).
- **Date generated:** provided by the owner as a completed 1024x1024 PNG;
  exact generation date not independently recorded, the owner-supplied
  source file's own metadata is the authoritative record if needed.
- **Date ingested:** 2026-07-15, via `tools/brand/ingest_hero_emblem.py`.
- **Output carries an invisible SynthID watermark.** Google embeds SynthID,
  an imperceptible digital watermark, in image content generated by its
  models. This is separate from (and not removed by) the visible generator
  watermark glyph this ingest pass patched out of the bottom-right corner.

## Generation prompt (verbatim, as supplied by the owner)

> Refine the attached emblem design. Keep the exact same style, detail level
> and neon palette (magenta, purple, cyan over dark gunmetal): a circular
> chrome car wheel with dark tyre and neon tread glow, an embedded
> three-reel slot cylinder showing neon 7 7 7, arched text WE ROLL across
> the top and SPINNERS across the bottom. Composition changes only:
> perfectly centred, square 1:1 format, the complete emblem fully inside
> frame with an even margin on all sides, no letters cropped, and the
> background replaced with a single flat, very dark plain colour
> (near-black, #0a0a12), completely empty: no city, no rain, no signs, no
> cars, no reflections, no other elements of any kind.

## Usage terms (fetched and archived in-repo, not on the owner's Desktop)

Per the standing convention that public tool terms are auto-archived
in-repo by builder sessions at adoption time (owner-held archives are
reserved for purchase receipts and paid licence documents), the governing
Google terms were fetched fresh on 2026-07-15 and saved verbatim (HTML
stripped to plain text, page chrome/nav retained for context) under
`docs/licences/google-gemini/2026-07-15/`:

- `google-terms-of-service.txt` (fetched from
  `https://policies.google.com/terms`) - the current, binding Google Terms
  of Service. As of 22 May 2024 this document directly covers generative AI
  use (see its "Respect others" / "Don't abuse our services" sections),
  superseding the additional terms below for non-business-partner use.
- `generative-ai-additional-terms.txt` (fetched from
  `https://policies.google.com/terms/generative-ai`) - retained for record;
  the page itself states it no longer applies as of 22 May 2024 except to
  business partners with a signed agreement referencing it.
- `generative-ai-prohibited-use-policy.txt` (fetched from
  `https://policies.google.com/terms/generative-ai/use-policy`) - the
  current Prohibited Use Policy (last modified 17 December 2024), still in
  force and referenced by the main Terms of Service.

**Gambling/betting/wagering scan:** all three documents were scanned for
any clause mentioning gambling, betting or wagering. None was found in any
of the three. The closest applicable provisions are general-purpose, not
gambling-specific: the Prohibited Use Policy's ban on "illegal activities or
violations of law", and the main Terms of Service's bans on "misleading
others into thinking that generative AI content was created by a human" and
"using AI-generated content from our services to develop machine learning
models or related AI technology". No explicit permission or prohibition
covering real-money gambling use was found; this is recorded here as an
open compliance question rather than assumed either way, consistent with
the project's standing prohibited-tool register practice.
